## Learning Objectives

All lectures support the course's 10 learning outcomes:

1. Use a basic vocabulary of interactive media to both give and respond to critique productively.
2. Create compelling interactive experiences through more careful and inspired interpretation/translation of content
3. Demonstrate an understanding of the iterative making process in interaction design
4. Conceptualize a product, object, or experience for the web and realize it through coding.
5. Evaluate the difference in designing interfaces for different kinds of devices
6. Evaluate how typography and its variables are applied to interactive systems
7. Research historic and current design precedents to contextualize your own work.
8. Be able to archive and document work that is printed, on screen or time based in a reflective manner for learning portfolio.
9. Combine your artistic creativity with technology related to the internet.
10. Demonstrate a comprehension of skills, methods, techniques and processes to realize interactive systems

## Format

Each lecture includes:
- Learning objectives mapped to course outcomes
- Topics covered
- Key concepts
- Code examples (where applicable)
- Activities and exercises
- Resources for further learning
- Preview of upcoming work
