Course Overview

CD Foundations: Interaction is designed to introduce students to programming as a creative medium‚Äîas a way of making and exploring. The coursework focuses on developing a vocabulary of interaction design principles which can then be applied across a range of platforms.

Students are encouraged to experiment with various media, tools, and techniques, ultimately producing a portfolio of interactive and visual projects designed for the screen. An emphasis is placed on typography as it applies to a screen context, research-based problem solving and a learning-through-making approach to technical skill building.

Throughout the semester, we'll explore historical and current interaction design precedents, learn the languages of the web (HTML, CSS, JavaScript), and build a foundation for creating compelling digital experiences.

** **

Learning Outcomes

By the successful completion of this course, students will be able to:

  1. Use a basic vocabulary of interactive media to both give and respond to critique productively
  2. Create compelling interactive experiences through more careful and inspired interpretation/translation of content
  3. Demonstrate an understanding of the iterative making process in interaction design
  4. Conceptualize a product, object, or experience for the web and realize it through coding
  5. Evaluate the difference in designing interfaces for different kinds of devices
  6. Evaluate how typography and its variables are applied to interactive systems
  7. Research historic and current design precedents to contextualize your own work
  8. Archive and document work in a reflective manner for learning portfolio
  9. Combine artistic creativity with technology related to the internet
  10. Demonstrate comprehension of skills, methods, techniques and processes to realize interactive systems

Course Structure

The course is divided into four units:

  • Unit 1 (Weeks 1-4): Working Methods - Tools, file management, HTML/CSS basics
  • Unit 2 (Weeks 5-8): Digital Canvas - Typography, CSS, layout techniques
  • Unit 3 (Weeks 9-11): Designing for Interaction - JavaScript, user interactions, multi-page systems
  • Unit 4 (Weeks 12-15): Networks - Deployment, accessibility, connecting to web services

Major Assignments

Throughout the semester, you'll complete a series of projects that build on each other:

  • Interview - Partner interview website (15%)
  • All HTML - Exploring HTML without CSS (Unit 1)
  • Expressive Text - Scrolling text experience (Unit 1)
  • Flags - Recreate flags with code (15%)
  • 25 Variations - Typographic explorations (Unit 2)
  • Stories as Networks - Borges interpretation (25%)
  • Living Collection - Dynamic collection website (30%)

Note: Attendance & Class Participation accounts for 15% of your grade.
