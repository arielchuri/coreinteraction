# Steps for Creating a Website (Student Project)

1. **Define Purpose** — Clarify what the site is about and who it's for
2. **Plan Structure** — Outline pages, sections, and content hierarchy
3. **Sketch/Wireframe** — Map out layouts (use Figma or paper)
4. **Design** — Create visual mockups (colors, typography, imagery)
5. **Build HTML** — Code the structure and content
6. **Style with CSS** — Add layout, spacing, colors, fonts
7. **Add Interactivity** — Implement JavaScript features (scrolling, animations, clicks)
8. **Test** — Check functionality across browsers and devices
9. **Optimize** — Improve performance and accessibility
10. **Deploy** — Publish the site online
11. **Review & Iterate** — Gather feedback and refine

## Website Creation Process

**1. Define the Creative Brief** [08:04 - 10:49]
   - Answer foundational questions: Why are you making the portfolio? Who is it for?
   - Consider multiple audiences (colleagues, friends, family, professional contacts)
   - Identify if the portfolio has multiple purposes (showing work/capabilities vs. personal aspects)
   - Document these questions even if not all are answered yet—this is the beginning of the design process

**2. Conduct a Content Audit** [11:01 - 11:34]
   - List all content you currently have to show
   - Identify content you want to add soon (that you need to create)
   - Align content with aspects of the creative brief
   - Recognize that you may build the framework now and populate it over time

**3. Develop Your Style Approach** [12:10 - 13:32]
   - Balance uniqueness with audience expectations
   - Consider whether a minimalist approach (simple, clean) or a more individualized design works best
   - Research exemplary portfolios as references or mood boards for inspiration
   - Gather screenshots of work you like to inform your visual direction

**4. Plan Interactivity and Features** [13:44 - 14:08]
   - Identify any interactive elements or special features you want to add

**5. Select Tools and Frameworks** [14:08 - 14:34]
   - Research and choose appropriate tools or frameworks that support your needs
   - Consider solutions that handle mobile responsiveness and other features beyond hand-coding
   - Discuss framework options in upcoming class sessions

This structured approach ensures you have clarity on purpose and audience before diving into design and technical implementation.
