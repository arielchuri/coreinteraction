[**Course Overview**]{style="font-size: var(--font-size-large);"}

CD Foundations: Interaction is designed to introduce students to
programming as a creative medium---as a way of making and exploring. The
coursework focuses on developing a vocabulary of interaction design
principles which can then be applied across a range of
platforms.

[Students are encouraged to experiment with various media, tools, and
techniques, ultimately producing a portfolio of interactive and visual
projects designed for the screen. An emphasis is placed on typography as
it applies to a screen context, research-based problem solving and a
learning-through-making approach to technical skill
building.]{style="font-size: var(--font-size-base);"}

[Throughout the semester, we\'ll explore historical and current
interaction design precedents, learn the languages of the web (HTML,
CSS, JavaScript), and build a foundation for creating compelling digital
experiences.]{style="font-size: var(--font-size-base);"}

------------------------------------------------------------------------

[**Learning Outcomes**]{style="font-size: var(--font-size-large);"}

[By the successful completion of this course, students will be able
to:]{style="font-size: var(--font-size-base);"}

1.  Use a basic vocabulary of interactive media to both give and respond
    to critique productively
2.  Create compelling interactive experiences through more careful and
    inspired interpretation/translation of content
3.  Demonstrate an understanding of the iterative making process in
    interaction design
4.  Conceptualize a product, object, or experience for the web and
    realize it through coding
5.  Evaluate the difference in designing interfaces for different kinds
    of devices
6.  Evaluate how typography and its variables are applied to interactive
    systems
7.  Research historic and current design precedents to contextualize
    your own work
8.  Archive and document work in a reflective manner for learning
    portfolio
9.  Combine artistic creativity with technology related to the internet
10. Demonstrate comprehension of skills, methods, techniques and
    processes to realize interactive systems

[**Course Structure**]{style="font-size: var(--font-size-large);"}

[The course is divided into four units:]{style="font-size: var(--font-size-base);"}

- **Unit 1 (Weeks 1-4): Working Methods** - Tools, file management,
  HTML/CSS basics
- **Unit 2 (Weeks 5-8): Digital Canvas** - Typography, CSS, layout
  techniques
- **Unit 3 (Weeks 9-11): Designing for Interaction** - JavaScript, user
  interactions, multi-page systems
- **Unit 4 (Weeks 12-15): Networks** - Deployment, accessibility,
  connecting to web services
